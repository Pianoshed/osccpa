Ondo State Consumer Protection Agency Strengthens Ties with FCCPC

April, 16, 2025

In a strategic move to deepen consumer protection and promote fair competition in Ondo State, the Ondo State Competition and Consumer Protection Agency has undertaken a high-level familiarization visit to the Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC) in Abuja.

The visit, which marks a significant step in regulatory collaboration, was aimed at fostering knowledge sharing, replicating best practices, and exploring avenues for joint initiatives between the two agencies. The FCCPC, Nigeria’s apex consumer protection and competition regulator, plays a critical role in safeguarding consumer rights and ensuring a fair marketplace. Its mandate spans across key areas including consumer protection, competition regulation, and business education. The Commission is renowned for its robust mechanisms in handling consumer complaints, regulating market conduct, and educating both businesses and consumers on their rights and responsibilities. During the visit, the Ondo State delegation led by the Administrative Secretary Hajia Kudirat Ola engaged extensively with FCCPC officials on topics central to strengthening consumer protection frameworks at the subnational level. Speaking during the engagement, the Administrative Secretary, Hajia Kudirat Ola, emphasized their commitment to building a responsive, transparent, and effective consumer protection system that reflects global best practices while addressing local realities. "The visit has been an eye-opener," said one senior official from the Ondo State agency. "We now have clearer insights into how to strengthen our complaint resolution systems, raise consumer awareness, and ensure that businesses in Ondo State adhere to fair trade practices." The FCCPC lauded the initiative, reaffirming its readiness to support state-level agencies in advancing the national goal of a consumer-centric and competition-friendly economy. As more states take proactive steps toward building their regulatory capacities, partnerships like this signal a positive shift toward a more decentralized and efficient consumer protection landscape in Nigeria.
